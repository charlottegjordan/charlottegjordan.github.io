WIP: Rag Quilt


Update 6/9/25

I've wanted to learn to quilt for YEARS. I've always joked that it would be the final the last step in achieving my true, final form as a grandma trapped in a young person's body. Well give me a walker and call me meemaw! I finally decided to commit to learning to quilt at the encouragement of my grandma's neighbor in Montana, who showed me her incredible craftroom and stunning quilts after hearing that I was interested in learning the discipline (shoutout Sue!). I solidified my commitment by purchasing my first ever sewing machine, which had me giddy every time I looked at it for a literal week.




For my first quilt, I've been following this great tutorial I found on TikTok. My mom asked to have this quilt when it was done, and she showed me a couple pictures of quilts with color schemes and fabric patterns she liked, so I chose the scheme of this project around her inspo pics.


All the work I've done so far is cutting the squares, which is way more work than I thought it'd be, especially as a clumsy person who sliced her finger on the fabric cutter the first time she used it (all good now). I've been taking a break from this project since early Spring, at first because it was too hard to leave school and come home to work on it, then because I took the summer abroad. Looking forward to jumping back in as soon as I'm home!
