Photo Diary: Museum-hopping through Warsaw!

Posted July 22, 2025

Take a look at some of the amazing museums I got to visit during my summer in Warsaw!

Warsaw is an incredible city with a wealth of amazing museums, and in my time interning at POLIN Museum of the History of Polish Jews this summer, I had the opportunity to visit many of them. As a bit of a disclaimer, this photo diary will only feature the light-hearted museums I visited in Warsaw. This city has a rich and often tragic history, which I spent much of this summer studying. This blog doesn't feel like an appropriate place to explore the memory of Warsaw's tragedies, considering it's lighter tone. I also consider this blog a personal project and would like to keep the professional and academic work I did this summer largely separate. That being said, I absolutely recommend visiting some of the world-class history museums in Warsaw if you get the opportunity. Here are a few that I can personally recommend:

Now that that disclaimer is out of the way, here are some of the great museums Warsaw has to offer!


Marie Curie Museum

This is one of my new all-time favorite museums. Aside from my personal interest in the history of science, and aside from the excitement that comes with learning about a famous Varsovian while in Warsaw, Marie Curie was an exceptional scientist and a fascinating historical figure, making for a perfect museum subject. The exhibition was the perfect size and did an amazing job giving equal acknowledgment to her identities as a woman, scientist, and public figure, along with all the complex intersections of those identities. The exhibit humanized her while maintaining the enormity of her genius, and it shed light on the achievements of her daughters, about whom I knew very little. The temporary exhibition focused on Curie's daughter and her writing of her mother's most famous biography, which was a great complimentary topic to the main exhibit's content.

Core Exhibition

Temporary Exhibition

One of my favorite parts of the main exhibit's curation was the inclusion of clear partitions displaying pressed flowers. Upon first glance, I was confused by their presence. As it turns out, the flowers displayed were chosen for their symbolic meaning, as each virtue or emotion represented by the flowers correspondeded thematically to the point in Curie's life that was currently being discussed. For example, marigolds, which are meant to symbolize grief and sadness at the absence of another person, were placed in the section that described the untimely passing of Marie Curie's husband. This approach to curation is unique and incredibly compelling, and I found it to be a really meaningful, emotional addition to the flow of the exhibit.



Museum of Dollhouses, Games, and Toys

I am a big fan of dolls and collectibles, so I was really hyped to visit this cute little museum. The first section displays dolls coming from and representing different regions of the world, and while I found some of the African dolls to border on racial insensitivity, the rest of the collection was a fun way to visually compare and contrast geographic and cultural distance. Dolls are an unexpectedly great lens for situating history and culture!

The next section was a cute little room focusing on Polish toys from the past couple decades, a fun glimpse into the childhood of late and post Communist Poland children.

The next exhibit focused on religious dolls, which was a shockingly really fun and interesting topic for me. It was really fun to look at all the religions represented through dolls and dollhouses, and many of the toys were quite beautiful. They even had little Amish dolls!

Main Exhibit

Finally, I went through their core collection of dollhouses, which was SO CUTE! My absolute favorite dollhouses were the ones depicting the homes of people in specific professions, like the home of a tailor for example. Favorites are bordered in gold!

Museum of Warsaw

Warsaw has an incredibly rich and sometimes tragic history, and this museum had very creative methods of communicating that history immersively. I especially loved learning about the symbols and monuments of the city, like the evolution of the mermaid as an icon of Warsaw. I also loved the section that focused on the history of Warsaw through data representations.



Warsaw Arcade Museum

I am a HUGE fan of arcade games, and this was less of a museum and more of a big arcade, which was perfect for me. I tried out a couple newer age games, specifically Marvel ones that piqued my comic-nerd instincts, but they weren't really my things. I also tried a couple classics I've never played before, and even set the new record on Donkey Kong! That wasn't super impressive because that only required me getting past Level 2, but it still counts! My absolute favorites that have always been my favorites, and the ones I spent the most time playing, were Pac-Man, Ms. Pac-Man, and Galaga.



Warsaw Museum of Modern Art

The Museum of Modern Art is housed in a beautiful, minimalist building and contains some solid works from contemporary Polish and broader European artists. Modern art collections are always a bit of a mixed bag, and such was the case here, but I really connected with a few pieces. Something I noticed while walking around was that the addition of a sound element to the modern art on display was incredibly popular. Favorites are bordered in gold!



It has been one of the greatest privileges of my life to spend a summer in Warsaw, and I highly recommend visiting Poland and it's many incredible museums if the opportunity ever presents itself!
